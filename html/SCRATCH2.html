Joseph Williams

The name Joseph is of Hebrew origin, derived from the Hebrew name Yosef (יוֹסֵף), meaning "God will increase" or "God will add." The name reflects a sense of hope and divine blessing, as it implies that the bearer will be given more, whether in terms of children, wealth, or general prosperity. It is a name that appears throughout various religious texts, particularly in the Bible, where Joseph is a prominent figure in both the Old and New Testaments. In the Old Testament, Joseph is known for being the eleventh son of Jacob and the first son of Rachel. His story of being sold into slavery by his brothers, rising to power in Egypt, and later reconciling with his family is one of the most famous narratives in biblical tradition.
